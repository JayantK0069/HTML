Breakfast

Ingredients

  • Eggs
  • Milk
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Herbs

Instructions

  1. Whisk the Eggs: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk or cream, salt, and pepper until well combined and frothy.
  2. Prepare the Filling (Optional): If using filling, cook it separately in a pan. For example, saut√© vegetables until tender-crisp, or cook bacon or sausage.
  3. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat. You can lightly grease it with butter or oil if desired.
  4. Pour the Egg Mixture: Pour the egg mixture into the hot skillet.
  5. Cook the Omelet: As the eggs start to set, gently lift the edges with a spatula and tilt the pan to allow uncooked egg to flow underneath.
  6. Add Filling (Optional): Once the bottom of the omelet is set, add your desired filling to one half of the omelet.
  7. Fold the Omelet: Fold the omelet in half using a spatula.
  8. Cook for a Few More Seconds: Cook for another 30 seconds to a minute, until the omelet is set and lightly browned.
  9. Slide Onto a Plate: Slide the omelet onto a plate.
  10. Serve Immediately: Garnish with fresh herbs and enjoy!

Tips!

     Whisk the eggs vigorously for a light and fluffy omelet.

     Cook over medium-low heat to prevent burning.

     Add fillings like vegetables, cheese, or meat for extra flavor.

    Don't overcook; aim for a slightly jiggly center.

    Sprinkle with fresh herbs and enjoy immediately!

Lunch

Ingredients

  • Bread
  • Butter
  • Cheese

Instructions

  1. Spread butter on one side of each bread slice.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium heat.
  3. Place one slice of bread, buttered side down, in the skillet. Add cheese on top.
  4. Place the second slice of bread on top, buttered side up, creating a sandwich.
  5. Cook until the bottom slice is golden brown, then flip carefully with a spatula.
  6. Cook the other side until golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  7. Remove from skillet and cut in half. Serve immediately.

Tips!

      Butter the bread evenly for the best crispiness.

      Use medium heat to avoid burning the bread.

      Experiment with different cheeses for unique flavors.

      Add fillings like tomato, ham, or saut√©ed onions for variety!

Dinner

Ingredients

  • Chicken Breast
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • Lemon Juice

Instructions

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, mix olive oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Coat the chicken breasts in the mixture and let it marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat the Grill: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  3. Cook the Chicken: Place the marinated chicken breasts on the grill. Cook for 5-7 minutes on each side, or until fully cooked.
  4. Check for Doneness: Ensure the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature of 165¬∞F).
  5. Remove from Grill: Take the chicken off the grill and let it rest for a few minutes.
  6. Serve: Slice the chicken and serve with a side of vegetables or salad.

Tips!

      Marinate the chicken for extra flavor.

      Use a meat thermometer to avoid overcooking.

      Let the chicken rest after grilling for juicier meat.

      Pair with roasted vegetables or a fresh salad for a complete meal.

FAQ

What are the basic ingredients for an omelet?

Eggs, milk or cream, salt, and pepper. Optional fillings include vegetables, cheese, and meat.

How do I prevent my bread from getting soggy?

Use slightly stale bread or lightly toast it before adding the cheese.

How do I know when the chicken is cooked through?

Use a meat thermometer to ensure the internal temperature reaches 165¬∞F (74¬∞C).
